You know the drone's brain is the flight controller (FC). But where do we configure how that brain behaves? The answer: <KeyTerm en="Betaflight">Betaflight</KeyTerm>.

## What is Betaflight?

Betaflight is the **open-source flight firmware** used on the vast majority of FPV drones. It has two parts:

- **Firmware:** The actual software running inside the FC — it maintains balance and generates motor commands.
- **Configurator:** The **interface** you install on your computer, connect the FC to over USB, and view/change settings with (Betaflight Configurator). When we say "go into Betaflight," we usually mean this interface.

You connect the drone to the computer with a USB cable, open the Configurator, and a **list of tabs** appears on the left. Each tab configures one aspect of the drone.

<PartHighlight part="fc" />

The flight controller (FC) highlighted above is the hardware Betaflight runs on. It's what you connect over USB; all settings are written into this board's memory.

## The basic tabs

For a beginner, the five most important tabs are:

- **Setup** — The general status screen. It shows a 3D model of the drone; when you rotate the drone in your hand, the model on screen rotating too confirms that the sensors (gyro/accelerometer) are working correctly. You also calibrate the drone as "level" here.
- **Ports** — Determines which job each of the connection ports (UARTs) on the FC is assigned to. For example, which port the receiver is connected to is defined here.
- **Receiver** — Shows the signal coming from the radio. When you move the sticks, you **verify** here that the on-screen bars (roll/pitch/yaw/throttle) move in the correct direction.
- **Modes** — Where you set up flight modes and switches. Which switch will **arm**, and which selects Angle/Acro mode, is defined here (remember from the arming lesson).
- **Motors** — The tab where you test that the motors spin in the correct order and direction. **This is the place to be most careful.**

<Callout type="info">
You don't have to memorize all of them on day one. The logic is: **Ports** defines the connections → **Receiver** confirms the radio is detected → **Modes** sets which switch does what → **Setup** shows everything is sitting correctly → **Motors** tests the motors.
</Callout>

## THE MOST IMPORTANT SAFETY RULE: Remove the propellers

In the Motors tab you can test the motors by spinning them one at a time with a slider. Before you do this, there is one rule that **must, without exception**, be followed:

<Callout type="warning">
**REMOVE THE PROPELLERS before doing a motor test.** If the propellers are attached while you test motors in the Configurator, a motor may unexpectedly spin up, and the spinning propellers can cause serious injury. This is one of the most important safety rules in FPV and is never skipped. Propeller = blade; bare motor = safe.
</Callout>

With the propellers off, you can safely check the following in the Motors tab:

- **Is each motor spinning in the correct corner?** (Move the slider and see where the spinning motor is.)
- **Is each motor spinning in the correct direction?** (It should match the arrow direction in Betaflight's diagram.)

A wrong order or wrong direction causes the drone to immediately flip and crash on takeoff — far better to catch this on the ground, without propellers.

If you changed something on the connected drone, don't forget to save with **"Save and Reboot"** at the bottom right before exiting. If you don't save, your settings will be lost when you unplug the USB.
