FPV is short for **First Person View**, meaning **seeing through the pilot's own eyes**. In <KeyTerm en="First Person View">FPV</KeyTerm> flying you don't look down at the drone from above; you fly **as if you were inside the drone, sitting in the pilot's seat**.

How do we do this? We mount a small camera on the front of the drone. The camera instantly sends what it sees, wirelessly, to a pair of **goggles** you wear over your eyes. Instead of looking up at the sky, you fly by watching that live feed.

<Callout type="info">
In short: with a normal drone **you stand on the ground and look up at the drone above you**. With FPV you look **through the drone's eyes, as if you were inside it**. That feeling is exactly what makes FPV so exciting.
</Callout>

## The difference between a regular drone and an FPV drone

The "photo/video drone" most people know (e.g. a typical DJI Mavic) and an FPV drone are designed for different purposes:

| | Regular (photo) drone | FPV drone |
|---|---|---|
| How do you watch? | From a phone/top-down, looking at the drone | Through goggles, from the drone's point of view |
| Good for? | Stable, calm footage; easy to use | Speed, acrobatics, the feeling of being inside it |
| Automatic assistance | Lots (self-stabilizes, holds position in the air) | Usually little; the pilot has more control |
| Learning | Easier | Takes more practice (but it's a lot of fun!) |

<Callout type="tip">
If you're just starting out, don't worry: FPV isn't scary just because it's "harder." If you learn it in the right order and **start in a simulator first**, you'll progress comfortably, step by step. This curriculum exists for exactly that.
</Callout>

## The parts that make up an FPV setup

At its most basic, an FPV flight needs three things. (We'll examine each one individually, on a 3D model, in the Hardware track.)

1. **The drone itself** — It carries the frame, motors, propellers, the flight controller (FC) that is its brain, and the battery.
2. **Camera + video transmitter (VTX)** — Captures the image and sends it wirelessly.
3. **Goggles + radio** — You see with the goggles and fly with the radio.

<PartHighlight part="fpv-camera" />

The camera above is the drone's "eye": it feeds everything it sees, in real time, to the <KeyTerm en="video transmitter">video transmitter (VTX)</KeyTerm>.

<PartHighlight part="goggles" />

The goggles are your "screen": they pick up the image the VTX sends through the air and bring it right in front of your eyes. This is exactly where the name "FPV" comes from.

## Why do we learn this first?

Because all of FPV's logic rests on this idea of "seeing through the drone's eyes." In the next lesson we'll look at the difference between **flying by eye (line-of-sight) and flying FPV**, and why that difference requires a **spotter** — both for safety and for the rules in Türkiye.
