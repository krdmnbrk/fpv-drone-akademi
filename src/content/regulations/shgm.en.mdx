# Before You Fly FPV in Türkiye: SHGM / İHA Rules

Flying a drone (officially called an İHA — **İnsansız Hava Aracı**, Unmanned Aerial Vehicle) in Türkiye is governed by rules. The authority that sets these rules is **SHGM** (Sivil Havacılık Genel Müdürlüğü, the Directorate General of Civil Aviation). The summary below is for **beginners flying FPV for hobby/sport purposes**.

<Callout type="warning">
This page is for educational purposes and is not legal advice. The rules can change. Before flying, always verify the current rules from an official source: the <KeyTerm en="Directorate General of Civil Aviation">SHGM</KeyTerm> website (shgm.gov.tr) and the İHA Registration System (iha.shgm.gov.tr).
</Callout>

## 1. İHA weight categories (by maximum take-off weight)

Under the SHT-İHA directive, İHAs are split into 4 classes based on their <KeyTerm en="Maximum Take-Off Weight, MTOW">maximum take-off weight</KeyTerm>:

| Category | Maximum take-off weight (MTOW) |
|---|---|
| **İHA0** | 500 g (inclusive) – 4 kg |
| **İHA1** | 4 kg (inclusive) – 25 kg |
| **İHA2** | 25 kg (inclusive) – 150 kg |
| **İHA3** | 150 kg (inclusive) and above |

A typical 5-inch FPV freestyle/racing drone (battery included, ~0.5–0.9 kg) almost always falls into the **İHA0** class. A small "tiny whoop" may be under 500 g.

<Callout type="info">
**The 500-gram threshold is critical.** İHAs with a maximum take-off weight **under 500 g** are not subject to mandatory registration (they are kept outside the scope of the directive). Registration is mandatory for every İHA of 500 g and above. Don't forget to weigh it with the battery installed; once you add the LiPo, most drones easily cross this threshold.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
**Two important points that apply regardless of weight:**

1. **No-fly / permit-required zone rules apply regardless of weight.** Even if you are under 500 g and have no registration obligation, you still cannot fly in no-fly zones (around airports, military areas, over crowds, etc.) and you must obtain permission in permit-required zones. "Registration isn't required" does NOT mean "you can fly anywhere."
2. **As of the 2025 revision, optional (voluntary) registration is available for sub-500 g aircraft.** To fly in a permit-required zone you must request a flight permit through the SHGM system; the permit request, in turn, requires you to have **a registered device in the system**. So even though it isn't mandatory, voluntarily registering your sub-500 g device is useful (especially if you'll fly in permit areas). Confirm the current situation at **iha.shgm.gov.tr**.
</Callout>

## 2. Registration requirement — where do you register?

Anyone who sells, buys, manufactures, imports, and **flies** an İHA weighing **500 g and above** must register with the İHA Registration System.

- **Device registration:** Both the aircraft's and the owner's/pilot's details are entered into the system.
- **Pilot registration:** Pilots who will fly an İHA of 500 g and above in Turkish airspace must also register in the system and obtain SHGM approval.
- **Where:** Registration is done through **iha.shgm.gov.tr** (the İHA Registration System).

**Age requirement (hobby/sport):** İHA0 pilots must be at least **12 years old**, and İHA1 pilots at least **15 years old**. For those under 18, a notarized undertaking from the legal guardian is required.

**Training:** For those who will fly the İHA0 and İHA1 categories **for hobby or sport purposes**, the directive does not impose a mandatory SHGM pilot training/license requirement; commercial use, however, requires separate training and documentation.

## 3. FPV-specific rule: the spotter requirement

This is the most critical point for FPV. The SHT-İHA rules require hobby/sport İHA0–İHA1 flights to be conducted **within the pilot's field of view**:

- **VLOS (Visual Line of Sight):** The pilot flies while maintaining direct visual contact with the aircraft, **without the aid** of any optical/electronic device.
- **Flying with FPV goggles is, by definition, not direct visual line of sight (VLOS)** — because the pilot looks at the aircraft through a camera/goggles, not with the naked eye.

Because Türkiye's rules require hobby/sport flights to be conducted within the pilot's field of view and treat FPV as being outside VLOS, **the most practical way to actually satisfy the field-of-view requirement is to have a spotter.** A **visual observer** (İHA gözlemcisi / spotter) who **continuously watches the aircraft with the naked eye** is both the most solid way to meet the visual requirement the regulation demands and the safest practice. The directive defines the İHA observer as "the person who will visually monitor the İHA and assist the pilot in conducting the flight."

<Callout type="tip">
In practice: don't put on the goggles and fly alone. Have an observer beside you who follows the drone with their eyes and warns you about people/obstacles/other aircraft in the area. Since Türkiye's rules do not treat FPV as flying within direct sight, a spotter is **both the safest and the most regulation-compliant way** to satisfy the field-of-view requirement.
</Callout>

## 4. Altitude and distance limits (İHA0 / İHA1, hobby flight)

When flying in places that do not require a NOTAM, are not no-fly zones, and have not been prohibited by the local senior civil administrator:

- **Daytime only:** Fly between sunrise and sunset, in visual meteorological conditions with **at least 2 km of visibility**.
- **Horizontal distance:** The İHA must be flown so that it does **not exceed 500 meters horizontally** and stays **within the pilot's field of view**.
- **Maximum altitude:** Do not climb above **400 feet = 120 meters** above ground level (AGL).
- **Distance from people/structures:** Fly at least **50 meters** away from people and structures.

## 5. No-fly zones

Without permission from the Directorate General, flying in the following places is prohibited **regardless of class**:

- **Around airports:** Regardless of altitude, in the area closer than **5 NM (≈ 9 km)** from the nearest runway edge.
- **Over crowded and very crowded areas** (residential areas, and places with dense crowds such as concerts/rallies/sports venues).
- **Prohibited, restricted, and danger areas:** Areas defined in ENR 5.1 of the Türkiye AIP (military areas, for example, are included in this scope).
- **Over private property** you may not fly without the property owner's permission.

<Callout type="warning">
Flying near an airport, in a military area, or over a crowd is both prohibited and very dangerous. If in doubt, don't fly; for zones that require permission, the application depends on the favorable opinion of SHGM and the relevant civil/military authorities.
</Callout>

## 6. Insurance

- **Hobby İHA0:** The directive imposes a **Third-Party Liability Insurance** requirement for İHAs **over 25 kg** and for İHAs used for **commercial** activity **regardless of weight**. No flying without insurance.
- A typical hobby FPV flight (small İHA0, non-commercial) generally does not fall under this mandatory insurance requirement; even so, it is recommended that you check the current situation and your local/personal insurance options.

## 7. Video and control link (RF)

An FPV drone uses two separate wireless links (RF, radio frequency), and their **power/license limits fall under the jurisdiction of BTK (Bilgi Teknolojileri ve İletişim Kurumu, the Information and Communication Technologies Authority), not SHGM:**

- **Video link (VTX):** Analog FPV almost always transmits on the **5.8 GHz** band; some digital systems also use this band. The video transmitter's (VTX) output power is measured in mW and affects range.
- **Control link (RX/radio):** The control link between the radio and the drone most commonly operates on the **2.4 GHz** band (e.g. the open standard **ELRS**). This band is an **ISM** (industrial-scientific-medical) band; in Türkiye the 2.4 GHz ISM band is open to use exempt from frequency allocation for devices that meet certain technical criteria.

<Callout type="warning">
**The permitted power and channel limits fall under BTK in Türkiye.** The maximum permitted output power and the usable channels for both 5.8 GHz video and 2.4 GHz control are determined by BTK's "Radio Equipment and Systems Exempt from Frequency Allocation" regulation and the National Frequency Plan. Before increasing VTX power, **check the current regulations from the official source (btk.gov.tr)**; exceeding the permitted power/channel is both illegal and disrupts other users' transmissions. (Use of the **900 MHz / 868-915 MHz** bands used for long range may be restricted/licensed in Türkiye; we cover this separately in the long-range lesson.)
</Callout>

## 8. Quick checklist (FPV beginner)

- [ ] How many grams is my drone with the battery? (Registration is required if over 500 g)
- [ ] Am I registered in the İHA Registration System (iha.shgm.gov.tr) as both device + pilot?
- [ ] Do I have a **spotter** beside me following the drone with their eyes?
- [ ] Is my flying spot more than 9 km from an airport and outside crowded/military/prohibited areas?
- [ ] Is it daytime with at least 2 km visibility? Will I stay under 120 m altitude and 500 m horizontal distance?
- [ ] Am I at least 50 m away from people and structures?
